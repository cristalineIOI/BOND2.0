What are cookies

Cookies are small text files that are placed on your device when you visit a website. They are widely used to make websites work more efficiently, provide a better user experience, and give site owners useful information.

Bond uses cookies and similar technologies (such as local storage and session storage) to operate, secure, and improve our Services.

How we use cookies

We use cookies for the following purposes: to authenticate your session, to remember your preferences and settings, to analyze how our Services are used so we can improve them, and to protect against fraud and unauthorized access.

We do not use cookies for advertising or cross-site behavioral tracking. Bond does not serve ads, and we do not share cookie data with advertising networks.

Types of cookies we use

Essential cookies: These are required for the Services to function. They handle session management, authentication, and security. Without these cookies, the Services cannot operate properly. These cookies do not require your consent.

Analytics cookies: We use analytics tools (such as PostHog) to understand how users interact with our Services, identify issues, and improve performance. Analytics cookies collect aggregated, anonymized usage data such as pages viewed, features used, and session duration.

Preference cookies: These cookies remember your settings and choices (such as language, display preferences, and interface configuration) so you don't have to re-enter them each time you visit.

Third-party cookies

Some cookies are placed by third-party services that appear on our pages. We use the following third-party services that may set cookies:

PostHog — for product analytics and usage tracking. Sentry — for error monitoring and performance tracking. Authentication providers — for secure sign-in.

These third parties process cookie data in accordance with their own privacy policies. We select partners who are committed to data protection and limit their use of your data to the purposes we specify.

Cookie duration

Session cookies are temporary and are deleted when you close your browser. They are used for authentication and maintaining your session while you use the Services.

Persistent cookies remain on your device for a set period or until you delete them. They are used to remember your preferences and provide analytics data. Our persistent cookies expire no later than 12 months after they are set.

Managing cookies

You can control and manage cookies through your browser settings. Most browsers allow you to view, delete, and block cookies from websites. Note that disabling essential cookies may prevent the Services from functioning properly.

To manage cookies in your browser, consult your browser's help documentation. Common browsers include Chrome, Firefox, Safari, and Edge, each of which provides cookie management in their privacy or security settings.

If you disable analytics cookies, we will no longer collect usage data from your sessions, but the core functionality of the Services will remain unaffected.

Changes to this policy

We may update this Cookie Policy from time to time to reflect changes in our practices or for operational, legal, or regulatory reasons. When we make material changes, we will update the "Last updated" date at the top of this page and notify you through the Services.
